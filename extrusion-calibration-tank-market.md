<p><ul><li>Vacuum</li><li>Non-vacuum</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Extrusion Calibration Tank market is segmented into two main types: vacuum and non-vacuum. Vacuum calibration tanks utilize a controlled vacuum to ensure precise shaping and cooling of extruded materials, enhancing dimensional accuracy and surface finish. In contrast, non-vacuum tanks use atmospheric pressure for the calibration process, typically being simpler and more cost-effective but may lack the precision of vacuum systems. Both types serve various applications, including plastic, rubber, and metal extrusion, catering to different production requirements and operational budgets.</p></p>
