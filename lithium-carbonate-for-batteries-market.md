<p><p>The lithium carbonate market for batteries primarily consists of two extraction methods: salt lake extraction and lithium ore extraction. Salt lake extraction involves extracting lithium from brine pools, which is a cost-effective method and provides a higher concentration of lithium. In contrast, lithium ore extraction involves mining hard rock deposits, typically spodumene, requiring more energy and processing but yielding high purity lithium carbonate. Both methods cater to the growing demand for lithium in energy storage and electric vehicle batteries.</p></p>
