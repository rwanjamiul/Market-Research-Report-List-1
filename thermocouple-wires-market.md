<p><p>The thermocouple wire market is categorized into several types based on the materials used. Copper thermocouples offer good conductivity but are limited to lower temperature applications. Iron thermocouples are cost-effective but susceptible to oxidation. Chromel, an alloy of nickel and chromium, is widely used for high-temperature applications due to its stability. Platinum thermocouples provide high accuracy and durability in extreme conditions but are more expensive. Other materials may include alloys designed for specific environments or purposes, fulfilling niche market needs.</p></p>
