Biodegradable starch polymers are designed to break down naturally over time, making them suitable for single-use applications like packaging and disposable items. In contrast, durable starch polymers are engineered for longevity and strength, ideal for products requiring extended lifespan, such as automotive parts and construction materials. Both types leverage the renewable nature of starch, contributing to environmentally friendly solutions in various industries.</p></p>
