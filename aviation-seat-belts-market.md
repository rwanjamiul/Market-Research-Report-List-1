<p><p>The aviation seat belts market comprises several types designed for passenger safety and comfort. Two-point seat belts, typically found in smaller aircraft, secure the occupant at the hips. Three-point seat belts, common in commercial airliners, add an upper strap for enhanced security across the shoulder and lap. Four-point seat belts offer even more stability by securing at four locations, often used in racing and specialized aviation applications. Other variations include harness systems and specialized designs for unique aircraft configurations, catering to diverse safety needs.</p></p>
